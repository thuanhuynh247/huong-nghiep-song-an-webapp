# Launch Playbook

## Primary Promise

Help users understand their interests and strengths through guided assessments, then turn the result into clearer next-step thinking.

## Audience Lenses

### Students 16 to 22

- need clarity, not jargon
- care about identity, suitable majors, and practical direction
- respond well to visual progress and shareable results

### Adults 23+

- care about career fit, transition, and self-understanding
- need a more mature tone and less school-oriented language

### Counselors, teachers, parents

- need confidence that the app feels structured and useful
- care about trust, clarity, and ease of guiding others

## Messaging Rules

- lead with usefulness, not psychometric terminology
- never imply the result is final truth
- avoid medical or diagnostic framing
- explain what the user can do next after seeing the result

## Suggested Onboarding Structure

1. Welcome and audience selection
2. Recommended assessment choice
3. Time and answer-format expectation
4. Quiz flow with clear progress
5. Rewarding result summary
6. Next-step CTA such as save, review, compare, or share with counselor

## Play Store Copy Shape

### Short Description

Clear career assessments and easy-to-read results for students, adults, and counselors.

### Full Description Structure

1. problem the user feels
2. how the app helps
3. supported assessment experiences
4. result and guidance benefits
5. trust and privacy notes

## Launch Sequence

### Phase 1

Private review with domain experts to validate wording, scoring, and interpretation.

### Phase 2

Small alpha with friendly users to identify drop-off points and confusing instructions.

### Phase 3

Beta with invited student groups, counselors, or partner communities.

### Phase 4

Public Android launch with Play Store listing, short-form content, and owned follow-up channels.

## First Metrics To Watch

- assessment start rate
- completion rate
- time to result
- save or share rate
- return rate within 7 days
- feedback themes by audience type

# Huong Nghiep Marketing

## Overview

This skill translates the app into a message people understand, trust, and act on. Use it to shape positioning, app naming, onboarding copy, Play Store messaging, and phased launch execution without drifting into vague or overhyped claims.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the task involves one or more of these:

- defining the app promise, audience, or differentiation
- writing onboarding copy, CTA copy, or result-explanation framing
- planning alpha, beta, early access, or public Android launch phases
- preparing Play Store listing text, screenshots, and launch assets
- improving activation, completion rate, or first-session experience
- deciding which channels should drive signups, feedback, and retention

## Workflow

### 1. Start With Audience Fit

Frame the product around real audience groups:

- students aged 16 to 22
- adults aged 23 and above
- parents, teachers, or counselors guiding someone else

Do not force one message for all three if the product experience differs.

### 2. Define The Aha Moment

The first-session win should be simple:

- user chooses the right assessment
- completes it without friction
- gets a result that feels clear, useful, and personally relevant
- sees an obvious next step

All onboarding and launch copy should support that moment.

### 3. Build Messaging Hierarchy

- Lead with outcome, not methodology.
- Explain what the user receives after completing a test.
- Use supportive, credible language.
- Avoid overclaiming diagnosis, certainty, or life-changing guarantees.

### 4. Optimize Onboarding For Completion

- One main action per screen.
- Reduce blank states and dead ends.
- Explain time commitment before the first question.
- Reinforce progress and completion.
- Make result pages feel rewarding enough to share or save.

### 5. Launch In Phases

- Internal review with domain owners
- controlled alpha with friendly users
- beta with invited students and counselors
- public Android launch

Treat every phase as a chance to refine copy, reduce drop-off, and gather trust signals.

### 6. Use Channels Deliberately

- Owned: landing page, email list, counselor network, school/community channels
- Rented: Facebook, TikTok, Zalo, Play Store search, short-form video
- Borrowed: partner schools, education communities, career coaches, youth organizations

The goal is always to move interest into a channel you control.
